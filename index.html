Hi! My name is Katherine and I am an American currently based in Utrecht, the Netherlands. I am a designer and researcher with a passion for cities. I believe that when the structured and analytical methods of social science are combined with the creative power of design, powerful solutions to seemingly complex issues in the built environment can be reached. This approach combines communication, graphic and analytical skills, which I have developed over the past six years in both professional and academic contexts. I have found this approach to be valuable on a number of scales and for a variety of projects. For an idea of what this approach can mean for you, please see the services below or some of my work.   • Collaborators: Deltametropolis Association, Ministry of Infrastructure and the Environment
  • Date: September 2012-September 2013
  • Category: urban landscape

As urban areas continue to require quality infrastructure, services and residences, so will the Deltametropolis demand an equally as high rural environment that ensures access to recreation, local food and green services, as well as water management and good air quality. What is the potential of the surrounding peatlands to match this need? As part of a larger research project at the Deltametropolis Association, I analyzed the future role of the Zouteveensepolder in Midden-Delfland. Research included exploration of ways to measure the intrinsic, economic and socio-cultural value of these types of landscapes. Interviews with experts and residents, analysis of policy documents, context and spatial analysis using geometric networks were used for the creation of an end publication.

  • Date: January 2016
  • Category: neighborhood regeneration

Vacancy continues to be both a physical and social problem within todayâ€™s urban areas. Alongside the obvious ‘waste’ of phyiscal space, a large number of vacant premises in a small area gives an impression of forgottenness and lack of care, leading to other issues, such as degradation, feelings of discomfort and further lack of local involvement. This problem is front and center in the neighborhood Laan van Nieuw-Guinea in Utrecht. By anaylzing successful cases with similar problems and visualizing the problem, we seek to find the most effective way to bring life back into the neighborhood, recognizing opportunities for collaboration and locations for potential temporary use. The second phase of the project will engage local owners, residents, and civil servants to create a platform where information about vancant propoerties can meet ideas and local initiatves.

  • Collaborators: Atomic Cities Research Group
  • Date: May 2012
  • Category: strategic regeneration

The decommisioned Paducah Gaseous Diffusion Plant was orginally used for the enrichment of uranium. Currently a 10 square mile underground plume of contaminants sits under the site. With funding from the National Department of Energy, our research studio spent 36 months visualizing the contaminated area and creating a strategic vision for the future use of the plant. As a Paducah native, I took responsibility for communicating with local parties and sharing the studio’s three years of work, which took the form of a traveling exhibtion consisting of past research, interactive models and possible alternative futures.

  • Collaborators: Pablo Moran, Research Masters Urban Studies Students
  • Date: June 2015
  • Category: urban governance

Across the globe, citizens, governments, private parties and academics are witnessing a multiplicity in approaches to urban development happening in their cities. Here top-down planning, bottom-up grassroots movements and entrepreneurial motivations collide, revealing conflicts, existing power relations and opportunities for cooperation. Who will make the city of the future? Dynamic, complex and ever changing, this question begs to be answered now more than ever. I was specifically responsible for leading a 15 person workshop, designing marketing materials, and organizing the theme and content of the resulting one-day symposium.
